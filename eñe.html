The eñe (IPA: /ˈeɲe/) is a letter of the Spanish alphabet.

The symbol on top (~) is called tilde, in Spanish also virgulilla.

It originated as a ligature of "nn", the tilde being a shorthand for a smaller n on top of the first one.

The sound is present in other Romance languages with a different spelling. For example, "gn" in Italian (gnocchi) or French (mignon), "nh" in Portuguese (amanhã), and "ny" in Catalan (Catalunya).
